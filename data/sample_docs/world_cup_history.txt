FIFA World Cup: History and Key Facts

Origins
The first FIFA World Cup was held in 1930 in Uruguay, which also won the inaugural tournament as hosts. Since then, the tournament has been held every four years, with the only interruptions being 1942 and 1946 due to World War II.

Most Successful Teams
Brazil has won the World Cup a record five times: 1958, 1962, 1970, 1994, and 2002. Brazil is also the only team to have played in every World Cup tournament since it began. Germany and Italy follow with four titles each, while Argentina and France have each won three times, most recently Argentina in 2022 and France in 2018.

Hosts Who Won on Home Soil
Winning the World Cup as the host nation is rare but has happened six times: Uruguay (1930), Italy (1934), England (1966), West Germany (1974), Argentina (1978), and France (1998).

The 2022 Final (Qatar)
One of the most dramatic finals in World Cup history, Argentina defeated France in a match that ended 3-3 after extra time, with Argentina winning the penalty shootout. Kylian Mbappé of France scored a hat-trick in the final, becoming the first player since 1966 to do so in a World Cup final, while Lionel Messi scored twice and converted a penalty in the shootout for Argentina, capping off his career with his first World Cup title.

Tournament Growth Over Time
The World Cup started with just 13 teams in 1930. It expanded to 16 teams for several decades, then to 24 teams in 1982, and to 32 teams from 1998 through 2022. The tournament format has grown alongside global interest in the sport, with the number of qualifying teams from each confederation adjusted periodically to reflect football's development in different regions.

Prize Money Growth
World Cup prize money has increased dramatically over time — from around $2.2 million in 1982 to a record $50 million for the 2026 tournament winners, reflecting the growing commercial scale of the competition.

Fun Facts
- The World Cup trophy itself (the current design, in use since 1974) is made of solid gold and malachite, and is not the same trophy the winning team keeps permanently — they receive a gold-plated replica, while the real trophy is returned to FIFA after each tournament for engraving and safekeeping.
- Germany holds the record for the largest World Cup win margin, defeating Brazil 7-1 in the 2014 semi-final on Brazilian soil.
- Just Fontaine of France holds the record for most goals scored in a single World Cup tournament: 13 goals in 1958, a record that still stands.
