FIFA World Cup 2026: What's Different This Time

Three Host Countries, First Time Ever
The 2026 FIFA World Cup is being jointly hosted by the United States, Mexico, and Canada — the first time in history three countries have co-hosted the tournament. Matches are being played across 16 host cities: 11 in the United States, 3 in Mexico, and 2 in Canada. Mexico becomes the first country to host or co-host the men's World Cup three times, having also hosted in 1970 and 1986. It is Canada's first time ever hosting or co-hosting the tournament.

Tournament Dates and Scale
The tournament runs from June 11 to July 19, 2026 — lasting 39 days, longer than any previous edition due to the increased number of matches. The final is being played on July 19 at MetLife Stadium in East Rutherford, New Jersey.

Bigger Than Ever: 48 Teams
For the first time in World Cup history, 48 national teams are competing, up from the 32-team format used from 1998 to 2022. This expansion means a total of 104 matches are being played (72 in the group stage and 32 in the knockout stage), compared to 64 matches in the previous format.

New Format
Teams are divided into 12 groups of four, each playing a three-match round-robin group stage. The top two teams from every group advance automatically, joined by the eight best third-placed teams across all groups — meaning 32 of the 48 teams progress to a newly introduced Round of 32 knockout stage, followed by the Round of 16, quarter-finals, semi-finals, a third-place match, and the final.

First-Time Qualifiers
Several nations are playing in their first-ever World Cup in 2026, including Cape Verde, Curaçao, Jordan, and Uzbekistan. Other notable returns include Iraq (first appearance since 1986) and the Czech Republic (first appearance since 2006).

Defending Champions
Argentina enters as the defending champion, having won the 2022 World Cup in Qatar. They are aiming to become just the third team in history to successfully defend the title.

Other Notable Changes
- Mandatory 3-minute hydration breaks are now enforced in every half of every match.
- Record prize money: the 2026 winners receive $50 million, up from $42 million in 2022.
- The final will feature the first-ever Super Bowl-style halftime show in World Cup history.
- As of late June 2026, the tournament had already broken the all-time World Cup attendance record, surpassing the mark previously set by the USA-hosted 1994 tournament.
